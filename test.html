Mental Health Resources

The WARN-D team has curated a list of reliable organizations in the Netherlands that offer support and assistance for various mental health issues.

Quick Access

  • Emergency: If you're in immediate danger, call 112
  • Suicide Prevention Hotline: 0800-0113 (free) or 113
  • General Mental Health Support: 0900-1450 (MIND)

MIND

MIND is a prominent mental health advocacy organization in the Netherlands. They focus on improving the lives of people with mental health issues through support, information sharing, and campaigning for better mental health policies.

Services offered:

  • Peer support groups and networks
  • Mental health information and educational resources
  • Advocacy for mental health rights and improved care
  • Campaigns to reduce stigma and raise awareness

Phone: 0900-1450

Visit Website

113 Suicide Prevention

113 Suicide Prevention is a national organization dedicated to preventing suicide through direct support, research, and education.

Services offered:

  • 24/7 crisis helpline for immediate support
  • Online chat service for those who prefer text-based communication
  • Self-help modules and resources for managing suicidal thoughts
  • Training programs for professionals and gatekeepers

Phone: 0800-0113 (free) or 113

Visit Website

Depression Association

The Depression Association (Depressie Vereniging) is a specialized organization focusing on supporting individuals experiencing depression and their loved ones. They provide a platform for sharing experiences, gaining knowledge, and finding support.

FAQ

  1. How do I know if I need professional help?

    If your mental health is affecting your daily life, relationships, or work, it may be time to seek professional help. Signs include persistent feelings of sadness or anxiety, difficulty concentrating, changes in sleep or appetite, or thoughts of self-harm.

  2. Are these services confidential?

    Yes, all listed organizations adhere to strict confidentiality policies. Your information and the content of your conversations are protected by law.

  3. What if I can't afford mental health care?

    Many organizations offer sliding scale fees or free services. Contact them directly to discuss your options. In the Netherlands, basic health insurance often covers mental health care, but it's best to check with your insurance provider.

  4. How long does therapy usually last?

    The duration of therapy varies depending on individual needs and the type of treatment. Some people benefit from short-term therapy (a few months), while others may need longer-term support.

  5. Can I get help in English if I don't speak Dutch?

    Many mental health professionals in the Netherlands offer services in English. When contacting an organization, ask about language options.
